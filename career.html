Career and Opportunities

Students who take the Information and Communication Technology (ICT) strand in Senior High School are prepared for various opportunities in higher education and the technology industry. The strand develops technical skills in programming, computer systems, networking, and digital technologies that are essential in today's digital world.

Possible College Courses:

  • Bachelor of Science in Information Technology (BSIT)
  • Bachelor of Science in Computer Science (BSCS)
  • Bachelor of Science in Information Systems (BSIS)
  • Bachelor of Science in Computer Engineering
  • Multimedia Arts and Digital Design

Career Paths After Graduation:

  • Web Developer
  • Software Developer
  • IT Support Specialist
  • Network Administrator
  • Graphic Designer or Multimedia Specialist

Students may also gain real-world experience through work immersion programs where they are exposed to actual IT-related tasks in companies and organizations. These experiences help them develop professional skills and prepare them for future employment.
